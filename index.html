Chapter 1: The Microscope

Understanding Candle Psychology

Many traders just look at "Red" or "Green." At Skillforge, we look at Intent. A candlestick represents the battle results of a specific time period (e.g., 1 hour). If the price opened at \$10, went up to \$20, but then closed at \$11, the story is that buyers tried to take control but failed miserably.

We categorize candles into three major types based on their Visual Energy:

TYPE A: MOMENTUM

"The Boss"

Full body, no wicks. This is the Algorithm saying: "I am going this way, and I am not stopping."

TYPE B: REJECTION

"The U-Turn"

A long wick sticking out like a needle. This means the price was rejected at a high level.

TYPE C: INDECISION

"The Nap"

Tiny body, long wicks on both sides. The market is resting or waiting for news. Do not trade here.

Why do Wicks happen?

In Synthetic Indices, wicks happen when the algorithm reaches a "Supply" or "Demand" zone and quickly fulfills all the orders. Think of a wick as a Price Magnet. Often, price will return to the tip of that wick later in the day to "fill" the empty space.

Skillforge Rule #1: The Body tells you where the price stayed. The Wick tells you where the price was rejected. Follow the body, fear the wick.

Section 1: Interactive Checkpoint

Situation: You see a massive green candle with NO wick at the top. What does this mean?

A) Buyers are exhausted.
B) Buyers are extremely strong and the trend will likely continue.
C) The market is about to reverse.

Correct Answer: B

Situation: You see a "Nap" (Doji) candle after a long uptrend. What is your next move?

A) Sell immediately.
B) Buy more.
C) Wait for the next candle to show a clear "Boss" direction.

Correct Answer: C

Chapter 2: The Macro Direction

The Staircase Blueprint

If candles are the words, Market Structure is the Story. The market never moves in a straight line. It moves like a person climbing a staircase. They take a step up (Expansion), then put their foot down to rest (Pullback).

The Bullish Staircase (HH & HL)

Skillforge Pro Detail: A Higher Low (HL) is "Safe" because the algorithm protects it. As long as the price does not drop below this point, the trend is BULLISH. We only look for BUY entries at the HL.

When the Staircase Breaks (BOS)

The most dangerous moment in trading is when the "Safe Low" is broken. We call this a BOS (Break of Structure). This is your signal that the party is over and the market is switching from Buys to Sells.

Section 2: Interactive Checkpoint

You see the price break a Higher High (HH). What is your next step?

A) Buy immediately at the top.
B) Wait for the price to drop back down to a "Safe Low" (HL) to find a better price.
C) Sell because the price is too high.

Correct Answer: B

The price just closed a candle BELOW the previous Safe Low. What do you do?

A) Hold your buy trades; it's just a fakeout.
B) Close all Buy trades. Structure has shifted to Bearish.
C) Buy more because it's cheaper now.

Correct Answer: B

The Skillforge "If/Then" Logic

When to BUY:

  • ✅ H1 Trend: Staircase is going UP (HH/HL).
  • ✅ Location: Price is at the "Safe Low" zone.
  • ✅ Candle: Rejection wick at the bottom or a "Boss" Green candle.

When to STAY OUT:

  • ❌ Candles: Lots of tiny "Nap" candles.
  • ❌ Structure: Price broke below the Safe Low (BOS).
  • ❌ Location: Price is at the very top (HH). Don't buy the peak!
